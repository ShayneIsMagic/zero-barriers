# Metrics & Statistics Guidelines
## Credible, Verifiable Claims for Zero Barriers Website

## ‚ùå DO NOT USE - Unrealistic Claims

**Avoid these types of claims:**
- "150% average growth in 90 days" - **Absurd and unverifiable**
- "Average growth" without context or attribution
- Time-bound claims that can't be proven
- Metrics without specific client attribution
- One-size-fits-all promises (e.g., "all clients achieve X%")

**Why this matters:**
- **Every client is different** - Results vary by industry, company size, starting point, and execution
- Damages credibility and trust
- Can create legal/ethical issues if questioned
- Makes the business look dishonest
- Visitors will immediately discount the claim

---

## ‚úÖ DO USE - Verifiable, Credible Metrics

### From Case Studies (Actual Data):

**1. Peak Growth Achievement:**
- **Metric:** "122% Peak Growth Achieved"
- **Attribution:** "SOS Support case study"
- **Context:** "In first year of transformation"
- **Usage:** Can be used with specific client story

**2. Growth Range:**
- **Metric:** "25-122% Revenue Growth Range"
- **Attribution:** "Across documented case studies"
- **Context:** Shows realistic expectations
- **Usage:** More credible than "average"

**3. Consistent Growth:**
- **Metric:** "25% consistent growth for nearly a year"
- **Attribution:** "Greg Williams, SeboDev"
- **Context:** Realistic, sustainable growth pattern
- **Usage:** Shows sustainable results

**4. Client Count:**
- **Metric:** "200+ Businesses Transformed"
- **Attribution:** General claim (if verifiable)
- **Context:** Shows scale and experience
- **Usage:** Can be used if accurate

**5. Success Rate:**
- **Metric:** "100% Client Success Rate"
- **Attribution:** "Across case studies"
- **Context:** If all documented clients succeeded
- **Usage:** Use carefully, ensure accuracy

## üéØ Best Practices

1. **Always Attribute:** Link metrics to specific case studies
2. **Use Ranges:** "25-122%" is more credible than "150% average" (recognizes client diversity)
3. **Show Real Examples:** "See how SOS Support achieved 122% growth"
4. **Be Conservative:** Under-promise, over-deliver
5. **Time Context:** If using timeframes, be specific: "122% growth in first year" not "in 90 days"
6. **Emphasize Individuality:** "Every client is different. Results vary based on industry, starting point, and execution."
7. **Show Industry Diversity:** Highlight experience across multiple industries (see Industries section below)

## üîç Verification Checklist

Before using any metric:
- [ ] Is it verifiable from case studies?
- [ ] Can it be attributed to a specific client?
- [ ] Is the timeframe realistic?
- [ ] Does it create realistic expectations?
- [ ] Would a skeptical visitor believe it?
- [ ] Can it be defended if questioned?
